On the software layer, decentralisation is evaluated by looking at the open source repositories associated with different blockchain clients, and specifically looking at their commit history. The projects that are currently supported are: <AppLink href="https://github.com/bitcoin/bitcoin">Bitcoin Core</AppLink> (Bitcoin), <AppLink href="https://github.com/bitcoin-cash-node/bitcoin-cash-node">Bitcoin Cash Node</AppLink> (Bitcoin Cash), <AppLink href="https://github.com/IntersectMBO/cardano-node">Cardano Node</AppLink> (Cardano), <AppLink href="https://github.com/ethereum/go-ethereum">Go Ethereum</AppLink> (Ethereum), <AppLink href="https://github.com/NethermindEth/nethermind">Nethermind</AppLink> (Ethereum), <AppLink href="https://github.com/litecoin-project/litecoin">Litecoin Core</AppLink> (Litecoin), <AppLink href="https://github.com/tezos/tezos">Tezos</AppLink> (Tezos) and <AppLink href="https://github.com/zcash/zcash">Zcash</AppLink> (Zcash). Specifically, the following metrics are presented in the dashboard: Shannon entropy, Herfindahl-Hirschman index (HHI), 1-concentration ratio, number of total entities.

Users can choose which options will be taken into account when generating the graphs.

In the 'Client Distribution' part, decentralisation is also evaluated by looking at the distribution of the blockchain clients themselves. With the exception of Cardano, the blockchain data that is needed to determine these distributions are collected using crawlers running on each network. The results are based only on data we have collected and do not include extensive historical data.
For the Bitcoin, Bitcoin Cash, Litecoin and Zcash networks, the crawler we use is based on the <AppLink href="https://bitnodes.io/">Bitnodes</AppLink> project.
In Ethereum's case, the network architecture consists of two distinct layers: execution and consensus. The dashboard reflects this architectural separation. The crawler used is <AppLink href="https://github.com/cskiraly/fast-ethereum-crawler">fast-ethereum-crawler</AppLink>.

Finally, for Cardano, the official documentation refers to only one client implementation, and the dashboard therefore displays it. Several other clients are in development, but not ready for production yet: <AppLink href="https://github.com/blinklabs-io/dingo">Dingo</AppLink>, <AppLink href="https://github.com/harmoniclabs/gerolamo">Gerolamo</AppLink>, <AppLink href="https://github.com/pragma-org/amaru">Amaru</AppLink>, <AppLink href="https://github.com/txpipe/dolos">Dolos</AppLink>.
