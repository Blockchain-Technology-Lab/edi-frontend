On the governance layer, decentralisation is evaluated based on the distribution of participation across the off-chain governance
processes of Bitcoin (BIP), Ethereum (EIP), and Cardano (CIP). Off-chain governance encompasses the informal and formal
processes through which protocol improvement proposals are conceived, submitted, evaluated, implemented, and adopted across a
structured five-stage lifecycle. The following metrics are presented in the dashboard: Gini coefficient, Nakamoto coefficient,
normalised Shannon entropy, and Herfindahl–Hirschman index (HHI)

We model off-chain governance as a five-stage lifecycle, as illustrated in figure below:

- **Stage 1** — Ideation (Formal Review): Author submits a Pull Request on GitHub; reviewers discuss and evaluate the proposal;
  the author iterates on the draft until it is ready to advance.
- **Stage 2** — Proposal: Formal submission of improvement proposals as pull requests, where editors assign an official proposal
  ID and the proposal enters the repository with a formal status.
- **Stage 3** — Ratification: The consensus process through which editors and the community formally accept or reject a proposal,
  reflected in commit activity and status transitions.
- **Stage 4** — Implementation (planned): Integration of accepted proposals into protocol software.
- **Stage 5** — Adoption (planned): Uptake of implemented changes across wallets, clients, and tools.

Running concurrently with Stages 1-3 is Community Deliberation, an informal discussion process spanning off-chain platforms such
as mailing lists, forums, and social media (e.g. bitcoin-dev mailing list, BitcoinTalk, Ethereum Magicians).
