On the governance layer, decentralisation is evaluated based on the distribution of participation across the off-chain governance processes of Bitcoin (BIP), Ethereum (EIP), and Cardano (CIP). Off-chain governance encompasses the informal and formal processes through which protocol improvement proposals are conceived, submitted, evaluated, implemented, and adopted across a structured five-stage lifecycle. The following metrics are presented in the dashboard: Gini coefficient, Nakamoto coefficient, normalised Shannon entropy, and Herfindahl-Hirschman index (HHI).

We model off-chain governance as a five-stage lifecycle, as illustrated in the figure below.

- **Stage 1 — Ideation (Formal Review):** Author submits a Pull Request on GitHub; reviewers discuss and evaluate the proposal; the author iterates on the draft until it is ready to advance.
- **Stage 2 — Proposal:** Formal submission of improvement proposals as pull requests, where editors assign an official proposal ID and the proposal enters the repository with a formal status.
- **Stage 3 — Ratification** _(planned)_: The consensus process through which editors and the community formally accept or reject a proposal, reflected in commit activity and status transitions.
- **Stage 4 — Implementation** _(planned)_: Integration of accepted proposals into protocol software.
- **Stage 5 — Adoption** _(planned)_: Uptake of implemented changes across wallets, clients, and tools.

Running concurrently with Stages 1-3 is **Community Deliberation** — an informal discussion process spanning off-chain platforms such as mailing lists, forums, and social media (e.g. bitcoin-dev mailing list, BitcoinTalk, Ethereum Magicians). Data is collected via the GitHub REST API from <AppLink href="https://github.com/bitcoin/bips">bitcoin/bips</AppLink>, <AppLink href="https://github.com/ethereum/EIPs">ethereum/EIPs</AppLink>, and <AppLink href="https://github.com/cardano-foundation/CIPs">cardano-foundation/CIPs</AppLink>, and from the <AppLink href="https://lists.linuxfoundation.org/mailman/listinfo/bitcoin-dev">bitcoin-dev</AppLink> mailing list, the <AppLink href="https://bitcointalk.org">BitcoinTalk forum</AppLink>, and the <AppLink href="https://ethereum-magicians.org">Ethereum Magicians</AppLink> forum.
