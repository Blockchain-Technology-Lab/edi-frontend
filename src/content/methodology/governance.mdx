On the governance layer, decentralisation is evaluated based on the
distribution of participation across the off-chain governance processes of
Bitcoin (BIP), Ethereum (EIP), and Cardano (CIP). Off-chain governance
encompasses the informal and formal processes through which protocol
improvement proposals are discussed, authored, reviewed, implemented, and
adopted. The following metrics are presented in the dashboard: Gini
coefficient, Nakamoto coefficient, normalised Shannon entropy, and
Herfindahl-Hirschman index (HHI).
We model off-chain governance as a five-stage lifecycle: Stage 1
(Deliberation), Stage 2 (Proposal), Stage 3 (Ratification), Stage 4
(Implementation), and Stage 5 (Adoption). The dashboard currently covers
Stage 1 and Stage 2, with the remaining stages to be incorporated in future
updates.
Stage 1 (Deliberation) covers community discussion prior to and during formal
submission, including GitHub pull request reviews and comments on the
proposal repositories, as well as participation in associated discussion
platforms such as the bitcoin-dev mailing list and the BitcoinTalk and
Ethereum Magicians forums. Stage 2 (Proposal) covers the formal submission
of improvement proposals as pull requests to the respective repositories. Data
is collected via the GitHub REST API from <AppLink href="https://github.com/bitcoin/bips">bitcoin/bips</AppLink>, <AppLink href="https://github.com/ethereum/EIPs">ethereum/EIPs</AppLink>, and <AppLink href="https://github.com/cardano-foundation/CIPs">cardano-foundation/CIPs</AppLink>, and from the <AppLink href="https://lists.linuxfoundation.org/mailman/listinfo/bitcoin-dev">bitcoin-dev</AppLink> mailing list, the <AppLink href="https://bitcointalk.org">BitcoinTalk forum</AppLink>, and the <AppLink href="https://ethereum-magicians.org">Ethereum Magicians</AppLink> forum.
