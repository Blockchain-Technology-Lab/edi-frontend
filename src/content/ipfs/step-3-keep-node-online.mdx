Once uploaded, our team needs to be able to reach your computer over the network to retrieve the data. Since you're already running a full node, this should already be the case — just make sure your machine and IPFS stay running (don't close the terminal or the app) until we confirm we've successfully retrieved the dataset.

You can verify your data is pinned at any time:

```
ipfs pin ls --type recursive
```

**Behind a firewall, VPN, or corporate/university network?**

IPFS needs an open connection (by default, port `4001`) so other nodes — including ours — can reach yours directly. Home routers are usually fine, but office networks, VPNs, and hotel/campus wifi often block this.

Run this to check whether your node has any publicly reachable addresses:

```
ipfs id
```

Look at the `"Addresses"` list in the output. If you only see local addresses (starting with `/ip4/127.0.0.1` or `/ip4/192.168.` / `/ip4/10.`), your node likely isn't reachable from outside your network.

**If you're not sure, or think you might be blocked, don't worry about fixing it yourself** — just mention it when you email us (see the next step) and we'll work out an alternative with you, such as a pinning service or a direct upload.
